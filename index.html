Why Choose Us?

Fiberglass Mastery

At Baltic Industrial Composites, we take pride in our mastery of fiberglass craftsmanship. Our expertise spans the intricate design and fabrication of fiberglass structures, addressing the unique needs of the chemical, petroleum, and energy industries. With meticulous attention to detail, we bring your fiberglass visions to life, ensuring precision and excellence in every project.

Global Reach

Whether you operate locally or on a global scale, we provide comprehensive fiberglass solutions customized to your specific requirements. From manufacturing small reactors to creating large-scale, transport-challenging structures, our global approach encompasses the diverse needs of industries worldwide. Trust us to deliver innovative fiberglass solutions that transcend geographical boundaries.

Commitment

At the core of our service is a dedication to making your fiberglass experience seamless and inspiring. Our goal is to redefine your expectations in fiberglass design by delivering a highly personalized service. When you choose Baltic Industrial Composites, you're not just selecting a fiberglass partner; you're choosing a collaborative journey towards quality, innovation, and excellence. Connect with us today to explore how we can bring your fiberglass projects to fruition.

Precision Engineering with FEM Analysis

We meticulously perform structural calculations using the Finite Element Method (FEM) analysis. This advanced approach ensures the highest level of precision in our designs. Design and Material Standards

Detailed Design and Material Standards Compliance

Our design process is expertly managed using the AutoCAD design program, allowing for detailed and precise engineering drawings. We adhere to stringent international standards, including EN13121 (Europe), ASTM RTP 2000-1 (USA), and DIN 16965, ensuring our products meet the highest quality benchmarks. Additionally, we ensure the applicability of materials through rigorous control methods, overseen by material manufacturers.
